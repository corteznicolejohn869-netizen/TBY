The Art of Bespoke: 15 Expert Tips

Elevate your style and ensure the longevity of your custom pieces.

1. Prioritize Perfect Fit

The greatest advantage of bespoke is the fit. Do not settle for 'good enough'; work with your tailor to perfect the shoulder, chest, and sleeve lengths. Fit is paramount to style.

2. Invest in Fabric Quality

Choose high-quality natural fibers like Merino wool, Egyptian cotton, or linen. They drape better, breathe more easily, and last significantly longer than synthetic blends.

3. Build a Versatile Core

Start with classic, versatile colors—navy, charcoal grey, and black—before experimenting with bolder patterns. These core colors maximize your mixing and matching potential.

4. Be an Informed Collaborator

Understand the basics of construction (full canvas vs. fused) and common style details (lapel width, vent type). This allows you to have a productive conversation with your tailor.

5. Dress for the Season

Match fabric weight to the climate. Use breathable linen and lightweight wool/cotton blends in summer, and heavier tweed or flannel in winter for comfort and proper drape.

6. Always Hang Your Garments Properly

Use wide, shaped wooden hangers (cedar is ideal) for your jackets and trousers. This maintains the garment's structure, allows fabric to breathe, and discourages moths.

7. Give Your Garments a Rest

Avoid wearing a suit or blazer two days in a row. Letting wool rest for 24-48 hours allows the fibers to recover, release wrinkles, and prolong the life of the garment.

8. Brush Regularly, Dry Clean Sparingly

Use a soft garment brush after each wear to remove dust and dirt. Only dry clean when absolutely necessary, as the chemicals can degrade natural fibers over time.

9. Master Accessory Proportion

Ensure your tie width is proportionate to your lapel width. A skinny lapel pairs best with a slimmer tie; a classic lapel needs a standard 3.5-inch tie.

10. Choose Travel-Friendly Fabrics

If you travel frequently, specify a high-twist or crease-resistant wool. These fabrics naturally shed wrinkles, keeping you looking sharp straight out of the suitcase.

11. Detail the Trousers Break

Decide on your desired "break" (the fold in the fabric above the shoe). Options range from full break (traditional) to no break (modern), affecting the overall silhouette.

12. Guard Against Sun Bleaching

Keep your custom garments out of direct sunlight for prolonged periods, whether hanging in the closet or drying. Direct UV exposure can slowly fade the color of fine fabrics.

13. Look for Customizable Features

Don't overlook features like working cuff buttons (surgeon's cuffs), contrast stitching on buttonholes, or a special lining color. These details define true bespoke personalization.

14. Document Your Measurements

Always keep a written or digital record of your final, perfected measurements and specifications. This makes future reorders or adjustments significantly easier and faster.

15. Address Stains Immediately

For spills, gently blot the area—do not rub—and take the garment to a professional cleaner as soon as possible. The longer a stain sits, the harder it is to remove.
