Selecting Your Perfect Shirt Fabric

The fabric determines the shirt's performance, feel, and silhouette. Choose wisely based on climate and occasion.

Essential Shirting Weaves

Broadcloth / Poplin

Broadcloth (The Classic)

**Characteristic:** Smooth, fine, and crisp. A simple, tight weave that creates a flat, formal finish.

  • **Best For:** Formal business, black tie.
  • **Feel:** Lightweight and structured.
Twill

Twill (The Soft Performer)

**Characteristic:** Recognizable diagonal rib or weave pattern. It's softer, has a slight sheen, and drapes well.

  • **Best For:** Everyday wear, great wrinkle resistance.
  • **Feel:** Very soft, luxurious drape.
Oxford Cloth

Oxford (The Workhorse)

**Characteristic:** Distinctive basket weave, giving it a visible texture and heavier feel. Very durable.

  • **Best For:** Business casual, rugged environments.
  • **Feel:** Durable, slightly coarse, becomes softer with age.
Linen

Linen (The Summer Essential)

**Characteristic:** Made from flax fibers. Highly breathable, light, and naturally cooler than cotton.

  • **Best For:** Warm weather, beach weddings, casual elegance.
  • **Feel:** Crisp at first, softens with wear; wrinkles are expected.

Understanding Thread Count and Ply

Shirting quality is often defined by the thread count ('s) and the ply of the yarn (single vs. two-ply). Knowing these numbers helps you choose the perfect balance of luxury and durability.

Thread Count ('s)

80s: Good quality, durable work shirt.

100s - 140s: The sweet spot for dress shirts; soft, fine, and long-lasting.

160s+: Extremely fine and luxurious, but more delicate.

Ply (Yarn Strands)

Single-Ply: A single yarn is used. Common in lower-end fabrics.

Two-Ply: Two yarns are twisted together before weaving. Creates a stronger, softer, and more durable fabric. **We highly recommend 2-Ply for bespoke shirts.**
