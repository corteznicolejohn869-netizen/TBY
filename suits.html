Selecting Your Perfect Suit Fabric

The fabric determines the suit's performance, feel, and silhouette. Choose wisely based on climate and occasion.

Key Suit Materials

Worsted Wool

Worsted Wool (The Standard)

**Ideal:** Business suits, year-round use. It is made from long, tightly spun fibers creating a smooth, durable finish.

  • The most common and versatile choice.
  • High wrinkle resistance and great drape.
  • Excellent for structured tailoring.
Flannel / Tweed

Flannel & Tweed (Textured)

**Ideal:** Cooler months, academic or casual styling. Flannel is brushed for a soft, fuzzy texture. Tweed is rougher and known for distinct patterns.

  • Provides superior warmth and structure.
  • Heavier weight (often 12oz+).
  • Best for a relaxed, less formal look.
Linen & Cotton

Linen & Cotton (Breathable)

**Ideal:** Hot, humid climates or outdoor events. Both are light and highly breathable, offering comfort in heat.

  • Highest breathability, keeps you cool.
  • Prone to wrinkling, which is part of the charm (especially linen).
  • Suitable for casual or destination weddings.
Luxury Blends

Mohair / Silk Blends

**Ideal:** Adding a subtle shimmer. Mohair (from the Angora goat) adds luster and crispness. Silk blends enhance softness and shine.

  • Adds a distinct, luxurious sheen.
  • Mohair is excellent at shedding wrinkles.
  • Often reserved for high-end garments.

Understanding Super Numbers (S100s, S150s, etc.)

The "Super" number on a wool suit fabric refers to the **fineness of the wool fiber**, measured in microns. Higher numbers mean finer, softer, and more luxurious wool, but often with reduced durability.

Super 100s - 120s (Durable Workhorse)

**Microns:** ~18.5µm - 19.5µm. This is the **ideal balance** of durability and softness for everyday business wear.

Super 130s - 150s (Luxury Comfort)

**Microns:** ~16.5µm - 17.5µm. Noticeably softer and lighter. Best for professional settings and special occasions. Requires careful maintenance.

Super 180s+ (Ultra-Fine)

**Microns:** Below 15.5µm. Extremely fine and soft, reserved for very high-end garments worn infrequently (e.g., weddings, galas).
